Harpy Eagle Habitat

The Harpy Eagle inhabits the dense, tropical rainforests of Central and South America, preferring large, undisturbed forest canopies. It thrives in lowland rainforests, often nesting in the tallest trees, such as kapoks. The species requires vast hunting territories, relying on monkeys and sloths as primary prey. Deforestation is a major threat to its survival, shrinking its habitat.
